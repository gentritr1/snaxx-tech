# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective players and users discovering Arrows, Rowflare, and FJALË, along with people evaluating the craft and reliability of Snaxx Tech as an independent studio. They may arrive from an app-store listing, a shared game link, or a search for support and need to understand the products quickly, trust the studio, and find release or legal information without friction.

## Product Purpose

Showcase Snaxx Tech's shipped and upcoming products, communicate the studio's care for playful interaction and dependable engineering, and provide trustworthy support and public legal destinations for app releases. Success means visitors remember the studio's personality, understand what each product is, can reach the right product or support channel, and can locate app-specific privacy and terms pages directly.

## Brand Personality

Playful, inventive, and energetic. Snaxx Tech should feel like an up-and-coming team that enjoys making ideas tangible: confident about craft, curious about what comes next, and human rather than corporate. The emotional goals are delight, momentum, and trust.

## Anti-references

Stiff corporate seriousness; generic agency-template or repetitive card-grid AI styling; childish gimmicks; motion overload that distracts from products or blocks access to information.

## Design Principles

- Let each product carry a distinct spark while keeping the studio voice coherent.
- Show craft through real product assets and responsive interaction, not abstract claims.
- Pair playful energy with release-ready clarity, especially for support and legal information.
- Make exploration rewarding without making navigation unpredictable.
- Treat motion as personality and feedback, with a calm equivalent for reduced-motion users.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 Level AA. Preserve visible keyboard focus, logical semantics and tab order, readable contrast, and mobile-first responsive layouts. Every meaningful interaction must work without hover, and all non-essential animation must respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
